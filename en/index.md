## About

I am **Bi-Cheng Dong** (董必成), Ph.D., an Associate Professor at the School of Ecology and Nature Conservation, Beijing Forestry University. I received my Ph.D. in Ecology from Beijing Forestry University in 2014 and my B.S. in Biology from Northeast Normal University in 2009.

## Research Interests

My research focuses on **biological invasion ecology**, with particular emphasis on:

**1. Macroecology and Invasion Risk Assessment**
Using global and regional plant diversity databases combined with statistical modeling approaches to systematically elucidate the naturalization and invasion pathways of exotic ornamental plants in China, providing theoretical basis for early warning and management of biological invasions.

**2. Clonal Plant Ecology and Invasion Mechanisms**
Employing clonal plants as a model system, I integrate field surveys with greenhouse experiments to investigate: (1) how clonal integration enhances invasive plants' adaptability to heterogeneous environments; (2) the regulatory role of native insect herbivores in the establishment and spread of invasive plants.
